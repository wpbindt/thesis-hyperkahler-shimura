\item Remark 3.2.4 is incorrect. As a result, the proof of Theorem 4.2.4, which uses Remark 3.2.4, is incorrect as well. We sketch an alternative proof. Let $S$ be a connected $\QQ$-scheme of finite type, and $f\colon X \rightarrow S$ a smooth proper morphism whose fibers are hyperk\"ahler varieties. Consider the quadratic form $Q\colon \RRR^2_{\et} f_* \AAf(1) \rightarrow \AAf$ defined as
$$
    \alpha \longmapsto \int \alpha^2 \sqrt{\td_{X}}.
$$
Let $s \in S(\CC)$, and let $\lambda \in \QQ^{\times}$ be such that if $q_{X_s}$ is the BBF form of $X_s$, then $q_{X_s} \otimes \AAf = \lambda Q_s$. Let $q = \lambda Q$. Then for all $t$ in $S(\CC)$, the form $q_t$ is equal to $\AAf \otimes q_{X_t}$. When $S$ is geometrically connected, this follows from Proposition~4.1.8. Otherwise, one can find $\sigma \in \Aut(\CC)$ such that $\sigma s$ and $t$ are in the same component of $S_{\CC}^{\an}$, and conclude using Proposition~4.1.8 and Lemma~4.2.1.

Moreover, since $q_s$ maps $\HHH^2_{\et}(X_s, \ZZh(1))$ into $\ZZh$, the form $q$ restricts to a quadratic form $\RRR^2_{\et} f_* \ZZh(1) \rightarrow \ZZh$. This proves Theorem~4.2.4.
\end{enumerate}
